Archetype & Mythology

In Babylonian mythology, Tiamat is the primordial goddess of the salt sea, the chaos monster from whose body the world was created. She represents the raw, untamed power that existed before order - the chaos from which all patterns emerge.

TIAMAT's 7 cascade levels mirror the seven primordial waters, each with its own power coefficient. From Genesis through Transcendence, the cascade builds upon itself, feeding chaos into chaos until patterns spontaneously emerge from apparent randomness.
