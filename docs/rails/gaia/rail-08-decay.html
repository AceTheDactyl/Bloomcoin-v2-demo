Rail 08 represents DECAY - the natural breakdown of stack frames. Like autumn leaves falling and decomposing, stack frames that have returned their values are now deallocated, releasing memory back to the system.

Frame Deallocation Characteristics

During DECAY, stack frames are systematically freed:

  • Process: Stack pointer moves back, frames deallocated
  • Z-Coordinate: z continues rising (approaching K threshold)
  • Memory State: Local variables go out of scope
  • Order: LIFO - last allocated, first freed

DECAY corresponds to Late Autumn - when leaves fall from trees and begin decomposing on the forest floor. The structures that supported growth are released, their nutrients to be recycled into the next generation.

LIFO Deallocation Order

Stack frames are deallocated in LIFO order (Last In, First Out). The deepest frames (most recently created) are freed first, allowing the stack to shrink systematically.

This mirrors nature's decomposition - the newest leaves fall and decay first, while the older trunk structures remain.
