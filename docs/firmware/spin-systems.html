Spin Glass Materials

Spin glasses exhibit frustrated magnetic order—ideal for exploring complex energy landscapes:

Cu-Mn

Copper-Manganese

  • Canonical spin glass
  • Composition: Cu₁₋ₓMnₓ (x ~ 1-10%)
  • T_g: 10-100 K (concentration dep.)
  • RKKY interaction mediated
  • Well-characterized dynamics
Au-Fe

Gold-Iron

  • Noble metal host
  • Composition: Au₁₋ₓFeₓ (x ~ 1-15%)
  • T_g: 5-40 K
  • Giant magnetoresistance
  • Easier sample preparation
LiHo_xY_{1-x}F₄

Lithium Holmium Yttrium Fluoride

  • Dipolar Ising spin glass
  • Dilution-tunable: x = 0.05-0.5
  • T_g: 0.1-1 K
  • Quantum spin glass regime
  • Best for quantum coherence

Implementation Details

Spin Glass Implementation

  • Sample Preparation: Arc melting, annealing at T > T_g, quench
  • Characterization: AC susceptibility, DC magnetization, specific heat
  • Measurement: SQUID magnetometer, μSR, neutron scattering
  • Temperature Control: Dilution refrigerator for LiHoYF₄ (mK range)
  • Field Control: Superconducting magnet with persistence switch

NMR Implementation

  • Probe Design: Solenoid or saddle coil, Q > 100
  • Shimming: Active shim coils, auto-shimming routine
  • Pulse Calibration: Nutation experiment for 90°/180° pulses
  • Detection: Quadrature detection, 16-bit ADC, >10 MHz bandwidth
  • Signal Processing: Digital filtering, FFT, phase correction

Coherence Detection Protocol

TRIAD sequence detection via spin echo:

  1. Initialization: Thermal equilibrium in B₀
  2. Crossing 1: 90° pulse — tip magnetization to transverse plane
  3. Re-arm 1: Free precession τ — phases spread (below HYSTERESIS)
  4. Crossing 2: 180° pulse — refocus phases (above THE LENS)
  5. Re-arm 2: Free precession τ — echo formation (below HYSTERESIS)
  6. Crossing 3: Echo detection — coherence measurement (above THE LENS)
  7. K-FORMATION: Echo amplitude > 0.924 × M₀ indicates success
