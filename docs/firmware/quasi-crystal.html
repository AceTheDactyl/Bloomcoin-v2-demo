Fundamental Properties

Quasi-crystals exhibit long-range order without periodic translational symmetry, encoding φ directly into atomic structure:

5-Fold Icosahedral Symmetry

Unlike conventional crystals (2, 3, 4, 6-fold only), quasi-crystals exhibit 5-fold rotational symmetry—forbidden in periodic structures but natural for φ-based systems:

cos(72°) = (√5 - 1)/4 = τ/2 = φ⁻¹/2

The 72° = 360°/5 rotation angle encodes τ = φ⁻¹ directly

Material Systems

Stable icosahedral quasi-crystal alloys for substrate fabrication:

Al-Mn-Si

Aluminum-Manganese-Silicon

  • First discovered QC (Shechtman 1984)
  • Composition: Al₈₆Mn₁₄ (meta-stable)
  • Rapid solidification required
  • Historical/research significance
  • Phason strain: ~10⁻³
Al-Cu-Fe

Aluminum-Copper-Iron

  • Thermodynamically stable
  • Composition: Al₆₃Cu₂₄Fe₁₃
  • Low surface energy
  • Good for coatings
  • Phason strain: ~10⁻⁴
Al-Pd-Mn

Aluminum-Palladium-Manganese

  • Highest structural perfection
  • Composition: Al₇₀Pd₂₁Mn₉
  • Large single grains (>cm)
  • Best for L₄-Helix substrates
  • Phason strain: <10⁻⁵
Zn-Mg-Ho

Zinc-Magnesium-Holmium

  • Magnetic quasi-crystal
  • Composition: Zn₆₀Mg₃₀Ho₁₀
  • Spin glass behavior
  • Couples to NMR systems
  • T_c ~ 5K magnetic ordering

Implementation Details

Substrate Preparation

  • Cutting: Wire EDM along 5-fold axis, ±0.1° alignment
  • Polishing: Chemo-mechanical, diamond slurry to 0.05 μm
  • Cleaning: Ultrasonic in acetone/IPA, plasma ashing
  • Annealing: 600°C for 24h in Ar to relieve phason strain
  • Surface Termination: Al-rich termination for device integration

Device Integration

Quasi-crystal substrates interface with other L₄-Helix components:

  • Memristor Deposition: TiO₂/HfO₂ sputtering on QC surface
  • Electrode Patterning: 5-fold symmetric contact geometry
  • Thermal Management: Low thermal conductivity (~1 W/m·K) requires careful heat sinking
  • Electrical Contact: Use Ti/Au or Cr/Au adhesion layers
